From 500 Square Feet to $100M

Chi Ta is a Vietnamese immigrant, born in Ho Chi Minh City in 1984. His family arrived in San Francisco in December 1988 with nothing. Eight people shared a 500 square foot studio in the Tenderloin District. His parents worked $4.15 an hour at a restaurant ‚Äî seven days a week, 12 hours a day, 84 hours a week. The Fall

He sold his Lamborghini, gave up his downtown loft, and had $0 in his bank account. His fianc√©e left. Employees he trusted stole his leads and funneled them to a competitor. Then he listed a rental property on Airbnb as a last resort. It made more in one month than three months of long-term rent. He saw the opportunity. Within 9 months: 30 units, $2.4 million per year in the San Francisco Bay Area.
