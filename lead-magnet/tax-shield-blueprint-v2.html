The Tax Shield: Cost Segregation

Cost segregation is a tax strategy that accelerates depreciation on a property, letting you write off a massive portion of the purchase price in Year 1 — not spread across 27.5 years.

How It Works

A cost segregation study reclassifies components of your property (appliances, carpeting, landscaping, fixtures) into shorter depreciation schedules — 5, 7, or 15 years instead of 27.5. Combined with bonus depreciation (now permanent under the One Big Beautiful Bill Act), roughly 30% of the property's value becomes a Year 1 tax deduction.

The STR Loophole

Here's where it gets powerful: with material participation in a short-term rental, these losses can offset your W-2 income. This is the part most CPAs miss entirely. A traditional rental can't do this. An STR with material participation? The losses flow directly against your active income.
