Airbnb Cash-on-Cash Return: How to Calculate & What to Expect in 2026

By Chi Ta · Forbes-Featured Airbnb Investor · February 2026 · 8 min read

Cash-on-cash (CoC) return is the single most important metric for Airbnb investors. It tells you exactly how hard your money is working — and whether a deal is worth pursuing.

After managing $100M+ in short-term rental properties across 16 states, I've seen CoC returns range from 15% to over 100% in Year 1. In this guide, I'll break down exactly how to calculate it, what good looks like, and share real examples from properties I've built.

What Is Cash-on-Cash Return?

Cash-on-cash return measures the annual pre-tax cash flow relative to the total cash you invested. It's the simplest way to compare investment opportunities.

Formula: Cash-on-Cash Return = (Annual Net Cash Flow ÷ Total Cash Invested) × 100

For example, if you invest $81,000 total (down payment + setup) and your property generates $40,900/year in net cash flow, your CoC return is:

$40,900 ÷ $81,000 = 50.5% cash-on-cash return

Compare that to:

  • S&P 500 average: ~10%/year
  • Savings account: ~4-5%/year
  • Traditional rental: ~8-12%/year
  • Short-term rental (average): ~15-25%/year
  • Premium STR (our model): 35-50%/year

How to Increase Your Cash-on-Cash Return

  1. Lower your cash in — Use DSCR loans (10% down) instead of conventional (20-25%)
  2. Increase revenue — Add unique amenities (game rooms add $20K-$40K/year in revenue)
  3. Optimize pricing — Use dynamic pricing focused on max dollars, not max occupancy
  4. Reduce expenses — Negotiate vendor contracts, batch maintenance, optimize cleaning schedules
  5. Pick the right market — High tourism + low supply + STR-friendly regulations = higher yields
